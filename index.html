Car Accident Statistics in the United States

There are roughly 6 million car accidents that take place each year in the United States. The causes of these accidents vary. Drunk driving, speeding, texting, and more all play into these statistics. Welcome to our website, Bad Driver Data, which looks into each of the 50 states as well as Washington D.C. in an effort to analyze information on car accident data. Feel free to look around the website, where we explore in particular the causes of accidents resulting from drunk driving and speeding. You can also view data regarding insurance rates and total costs increased as a result of car accidents per state.

Speeding is one of the two major causes for car accidents that our statistics report on. It is regarded as the 2nd largest cause of car accidents in the United States. Drunk driving is also a large cause for car crashes in the United States and is the second major statistic that is covered within the data. Annually, there are millions of car accidents that result from both speeding and driving under the influence. Click on the Accident Data link above to learn more!

There are 50 states not including Washington D.C., and all have their own unique accident reports annually. Car insurance premiums and losses incurred by insurance companies per insured driver also fluctuate by state.
